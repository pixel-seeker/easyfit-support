Privacy Notice

Tabata HIIT Interval Timer — Last updated: May 28, 2026

This notice explains how Tabata HIIT Interval Timer handles information when you use the iPhone app, widgets, and Apple Watch companion experience. The app is provided by Sergei Melchugov ("EasyFit", "we", "us"). The app is designed so most training data stays on your own devices or in Apple services you control.

Tabata HIIT Interval Timer does not sell your personal information. HealthKit data is not used for advertising and is not sent to third-party ad networks.

2. Data handled by the app

Health and activity data

With your permission, the app may read or write Apple Health data such as heart rate, active energy, workout records, date of birth, body mass, and height. This information is used to show live metrics, estimate energy, and save completed sessions to Apple Health.

Onboarding and fitness preferences

The app may store information you enter during onboarding or in settings, such as goals, target weight, protected areas, workout preferences, coach choice, timer settings, reminders, and customization choices. This information is used to personalize the app experience on your device.

Apple Watch companion data

The Apple Watch app may collect heart-rate samples during an active session and exchange live session state with your iPhone through Watch Connectivity. Session status, haptic preferences, and display settings may be exchanged between your paired devices.

Session history and routines

Completed sessions, custom routines, milestones, preferences, and widget summaries are stored locally with Apple platform storage technologies such as SwiftData and UserDefaults. If iCloud sync is enabled, compatible app data may be stored in your private iCloud container.

Analytics and diagnostics

If analytics are allowed, the app may collect aggregated usage events such as screen views, feature usage, subscription flow events, permission status, device type, app version, and operating system version. Crash and diagnostic reports may include technical logs, stack traces, and app state needed to fix reliability issues.

Purchases

Subscriptions and purchases are processed by Apple. RevenueCat may be used to validate receipts, manage entitlements, and show current product offerings. The app does not receive your credit card number or App Store payment details.

Music access

If you grant Apple Music permission, Tabata HIIT Interval Timer can display or control playback during sessions. The app does not build a listening profile or sell music-library information.

Notifications

The app may schedule local reminders and Watch alerts if you allow notifications. You can turn these off in system settings or inside the app.

Support messages

If you contact support, the details you choose to send, such as name, device context, app version, issue category, message content, screenshots, or videos, are used only to respond to your request and improve support quality.

3. How data is used

Tabata HIIT Interval Timer uses data to:

  • run interval sessions and companion Watch features;
  • calculate session summaries and energy estimates;
  • personalize onboarding, plans, routines, coach prompts, and settings;
  • save activity to Apple Health when permitted;
  • sync your own routines and history through iCloud if enabled;
  • manage subscriptions and premium access;
  • improve stability and diagnose crashes;
  • understand aggregate feature usage when analytics are allowed;
  • provide reminders and support responses.
